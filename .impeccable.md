## Design Context

### Users
Blip serves people at crowded events who need reliable crew communication when mobile networks fail, plus event organisers evaluating whether Blip can support attendee comms, announcements, and safety workflows.

### Brand Personality
Sleek, event-grade, trustworthy. The tone should feel modern and capable without sounding corporate, gimmicky, or festival-cringey.

### Aesthetic Direction
Keep the existing black, white, and Blip purple palette. Move away from generic AI SaaS tropes such as heavy glassmorphism, gradient text, glowing pill-heavy UI, and repeated icon cards. Use a cleaner event-systems visual language: signal maps, operational proof, restrained contrast, crisp surfaces, and purposeful purple accents.

### Design Principles
- Lead with trust: show why the product works, where it helps, and what is protected.
- Keep it event-native, not novelty-themed: reference crowds, venues, crews, and organisers without kitsch.
- Reduce choice overload: make the primary action obvious and push secondary paths down-page.
- Use purple as a signal color, not decoration.
- Prefer concrete proof and product context over abstract marketing claims.
